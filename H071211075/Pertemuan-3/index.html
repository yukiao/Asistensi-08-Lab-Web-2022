ABOUT THE FILM

In the future, governments and economies across the globe have collapsed, food is scarce, NASA is no more, and the 20th Century is to blame. A mysterious rip in space-time opens and it's up to a group of explorers to explore and offer hope for mankind. Surpassing the limitations of human space travel and to conquer the vast distances involved in this attempt to save mankind from a dying earth.
The film is based on the scientific theories and script treatment of renowned theoretical physicist, Kip Thorne and screenwriter Jonathan Nolan. The original story was written by Christopher Nolan's younger brother, Jonathan Nolan. Initially The film was supposed to be directed by Steven Spielberg. But there's a schedule conflict and the movie ended directed by Christopher Nolan.

Because Kip Thorne was included in the making of the film, lots of the film's original idea didn't ended up in the final product. Because most of the original ideas are not scientifically accurate or not close to become a reality, even if it's still a theory until this date. Kip Thorne, which is also a famous physicist ended up writing a novel about the science of the film because of how accurate it is according to science.
Christopher Nolan is famously known for his practical effects. He always tried to do it as real as possible before opting for computer graphics or CGI. Lots of the scene are using practical effects. One of the very famous scene which is the cornfield chase was produced with no CGI. They grew 500 acres of corn only to film that particular scene. After the production ended they sold the corn for profit.
